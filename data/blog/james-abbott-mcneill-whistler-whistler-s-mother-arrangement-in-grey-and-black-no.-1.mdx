* *Title:* Arrangement in grey and black No. 1 (Arrangement en gris et noir n°1)
* *Artist:* James Abbott McNeill Whistler (1834-1903)
* *Date:* 1871
* *Made in:* London, UK
* *Medium:* Oil on canvas
* *Dimensions:* 144 × 162 cm
* *Location:*: Musée d'Orsay, Paris, France
* *Photo credit:* Musée d’Orsay, Dist. RMN-Grand Palais / Patrice Schmidt

"Arrangement en gris et noir n°1" (1871), more commonly known as "Whistler’s Mother", is one of James McNeill Whistler’s most iconic works and a defining statement of his artistic philosophy. The painting depicts Anna McNeill Whistler, the artist’s mother, seated in profile against a sparsely furnished interior. Her rigid posture, folded hands, and solemn expression convey a sense of gravity and quiet endurance.

Whistler deliberately suppresses anecdote and sentiment in favor of formal balance. The composition is structured by strong horizontals and verticals: the wall line, the floor edge, the curtain, and the framed prints create a measured architectural rhythm. The restrained palette of blacks, greys, and muted whites emphasizes tonal harmony over color, reflecting Whistler’s belief that painting should aspire to the condition of music—an “arrangement” of visual elements rather than a narrative scene.

Texture plays a crucial role: the softness of the mother’s shawl contrasts with the hard surfaces of the wall and floor, while subtle variations in grey prevent monotony. Although the subject is deeply personal, Whistler transforms the figure into a near-abstract presence, monumental yet intimate.

The result is a meditation on stillness, form, and restraint. The painting’s emotional power emerges not from overt expression, but from its disciplined simplicity and austere beauty.
